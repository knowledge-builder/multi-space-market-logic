## 4. Behavior through regime-based data

### What “regime” means in your system
A regime is:
- a temporary stability of structural relationships in rolling windows
- Not a label — a persistent configuration of measurements

### Function in your system
Regimes define:
- segments of time where the system behaves under a consistent rule-set

**Examples:**
- high scale / low distance -> churn regime
- low scale / high distance -> drift regime

### Why they matter
- **Without regimes:**
    - you assume one global system behavior (false)
    - you average incompatible structures (incorrect inference)
- **With regimes:**
    - you accept that the system changes its internal rules over time

This is critical for stochastic systems.
