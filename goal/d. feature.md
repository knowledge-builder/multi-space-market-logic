## 3. Feature Relationships

### What this really means:
Not “correlations between indicators”.

But:
- relationships between projections of different spaces

### Function in your system:
They define:
- the internal geometry of your measurement system

### Example:
- state features (distance)
- derivative features (scale)
- behavioral features (imbalance)

When you relate them, you are:
- building bridges between coordinate systems

### Why they matter:
- **Without this:**
    - features are isolated numbers
    - no structure emerges
- **With this:**
    - features become a connected representation space

**But danger:**
- invalid relationships create fake structure (this is the failure mode you’re trying to avoid)
