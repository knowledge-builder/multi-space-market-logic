```
1) large distance — large scale

(high displacement / high motion)
fast expansion / breakout
   
   • What is structurally true
   • distance large → system is far from its mean
   • scale large → system is moving aggressively per step

   • So:
   • displaced state + high motion
   
   • What this actually implies
   • the system is not anchored to equilibrium
   • movement is actively pushing the state further or keeping it unstable
   
   • Two possibilities exist (don’t collapse them):

A. Directional continuation
   • steps align → distance may keep increasing
   • this is what people call “breakout”

B. Chaotic expansion
   • large steps but alternating
   • distance fluctuates while staying large
   • still high energy, but not clean direction

• Key insight
   • you are observing energy + displacement, not “trend”
   • you still don’t know directionality consistency

2) large distance — small scale

(high displacement / low motion)
slow drift / trend plateau

   • What is structurally true
   • distance large → system is far from equilibrium
   • scale small → each step is small
   
   • So:
   • displaced state + low motion
   
   • What this actually implies
   • the system has already moved away from the mean, but now movement is weak or slowing
   • state is holding its position
   
   • Two interpretations (both valid):

A. Drift (creeping continuation)
   • small consistent steps in one direction
   • distance may slowly increase or maintain

B. Plateau (stalling)
   • movement too weak to change structure
   • distance stays large but stagnant

• Key insight
   • this is low energy at a displaced level
   • this is where people incorrectly assume “it must revert”
   • there is no force implied—only low motion

3) small distance — large scale

(low displacement / high motion)
violent churn near equilibrium
   
   • What is structurally true
   • distance small → system is near mean
   • scale large → large step movements
   
   • So:
   • centered state + high motion
   
   • What this actually implies
   • price is constantly moving away and snapping back
   • movements cancel out in net displacement
   
   • Internal structure
   • high local variance
   • low net displacement
   • frequent sign changes in Δclose

• Key insight
   • energy is high, but direction is incoherent
   • this is a high-noise regime
   • this is NOT “safe”
   • it is structurally unstable

4) small distance — small scale

(low displacement / low motion)
stable equilibrium
   
   • What is structurally true
   • distance small → near equilibrium
   • scale small → low movement
   
   • So:
   • centered state + low motion
   
   • What this actually implies
   • system is quiet
   • minimal displacement and minimal activity
   
   • Internal structure
   • small step changes
   • low variance
   • high persistence of state

• Key insight
   • this is low energy, low displacement
   • this is the closest thing to “stable” in your framework
```
